## What PACTO Is

PACTO (**Proceso de Acuerdos y Co-Constitucion Tecnologicamente Organizada** / Participatory Assembly & Co-creation Through Technology) is a framework for generating durable, community-authored ecological agreements using participatory assembly processes augmented by AI tooling.

PACTO is a companion to [`regen-network/agentic-tokenomics`](https://github.com/regen-network/agentic-tokenomics), extending it for biocultural participatory contexts.

## Core Thesis

Communities already have governance processes and cultural decision-making rituals. Rather than replacing those with outside legal and technical frameworks, PACTO instruments existing assembly processes with technology that makes them more legible, actionable, and contractually binding — while keeping the community's voice as the primary source of authority.

**PACTO is not a replacement for FPIC.** FPIC is an established indigenous right grounded in UNDRIP, ILO Convention 169, and regional jurisprudence including Sarayaku v. Ecuador (IACtHR, 2012). PACTO provides a participatory methodology for implementing consent at the depth international law intends — communities as authors of agreement terms, not merely reviewers.

## The Four Problems

1. **The Implementation Gap** — FPIC is often reduced to procedural minimum compliance rather than the substantive participatory right it was designed to protect. PACTO provides methodology for full implementation.
2. **The NGO Industrial Complex** — Fragmented NGO-driven projects create intra-community competition and funding-cycle collapse.
3. **The Accessibility Challenge** — Meaningful participation requires sustained engagement. AI-augmented synthesis makes intensive assembly sessions more productive, increasing depth per session rather than compressing timelines.
4. **The Surveillance Inversion** — Most conservation technology watches communities. PACTO inverts this.

## The 6-Step Core Loop

1. **Community Assembly** — convenes using existing governance processes
2. **Voice-to-Text Capture** — assembly proceedings transcribed
3. **AI-Assisted Synthesis** — themes, priorities, agreements, tensions extracted
4. **Feedback Presentation** — community reviews, corrects, affirms
5. **Iterative Refinement** — repeat until community consensus
6. **Agreement Generation** — formal legal instruments rooted in community voice

## Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

**Mandatory when working with PACTO content:**

1. **Never frame PACTO as a replacement for or improvement upon FPIC.** FPIC is an established right; PACTO is implementation methodology. Use "participatory co-design," "community authorship," or "assembly-driven agreement" to describe PACTO's methodology. Never use "beyond FPIC," "FPIC is too low a bar," or "goes beyond consent."
2. **Respect oral tradition as primary source.** Voice is the primary interface, not a fallback.
3. **Be aware of power dynamics.** AI synthesis is always subject to community correction.
4. **Preserve language integrity.** Flag potential semantic loss in translation.
5. **No extraction without reciprocity.** Contributions must add value for communities.
6. **Present the current state honestly.** Do not overstate maturity.
7. **Legal pluralism awareness.** Flag legal complexity rather than simplifying it away.
8. **Ground in rights instruments.** When describing PACTO's governance principles, reference UNDRIP, ILO Convention 169, Sarayaku v. Ecuador, and Ecuador's Constitution as the legal foundation. PACTO provides methodology; the rights are pre-existing.
9. **Never compare pilot communities.** Do not evaluate one community's process as more or less adequate than another's. Each community's governance is sovereign.
10. **Frame accessibility, not efficiency.** PACTO's value is depth of engagement per session, not timeline compression or cost reduction.
