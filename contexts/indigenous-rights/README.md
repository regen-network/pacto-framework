# Context: Indigenous Rights

This is PACTO's primary and most developed context module. It provides the rights-grounded implementation of PACTO methodology for indigenous peoples and local communities, particularly in the Amazon Basin.

---

## Rights Foundation

PACTO's indigenous rights context is grounded in pre-existing legal instruments:

- **UNDRIP** (2007) — self-determination, participation in decision-making, FPIC, land rights, cultural heritage
- **ILO Convention 169** (1989) — consultation in good faith, right to decide development priorities, natural resource rights
- **Sarayaku v. Ecuador** (IACtHR, 2012) — consultation must be prior, culturally appropriate, in good faith, and oriented toward consent
- **Ecuador Constitution** (2008) — collective rights, Rights of Nature, environmental consultation

PACTO does not create these rights. It provides methodology for their full exercise.

## Relationship to Core

This context module builds on top of the universal PACTO methodology defined in [`core/`](../../core/). The core provides the 6-step Core Loop, evidence trail, mechanism overview, governance principles, and sovereign infrastructure specifications. This context adds:

- **Legal grounding** in international indigenous rights law
- **Cultural protocols** for assembly, voice capture, and AI synthesis in indigenous contexts
- **Field experience** from Amazon Basin pilots
- **Biocultural credit design** bridging ecological and cultural values
- **Process integrity requirements** specific to indigenous knowledge
