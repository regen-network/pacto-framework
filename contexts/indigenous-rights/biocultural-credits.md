# Biocultural Credits

## What Makes a Credit "Biocultural"

A credit earns "biocultural" status when it meets **both** conditions:

1. **Ecological outcomes** — measurable environmental benefits (carbon sequestration, biodiversity conservation, habitat quality) verified through standard MRV
2. **Participatory process** — community authorship of agreement terms, demonstrated through the Evidence Trail

Neither alone is sufficient. Ecological outcomes without community authorship are conventional credits. Community participation without ecological measurement is governance documentation, not a tradeable credit.

---

## The Biocultural Premium

Biocultural credits are expected to command a premium over conventional ecological credits because they:

- **Demonstrate genuine community benefit** — not just consent, but authorship
- **Carry lower reversal risk** — community-authored agreements have higher durability because communities have genuine ownership of the terms
- **Meet emerging ESG requirements** — TNFD (Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures) and SBTi (Science Based Targets initiative) increasingly require demonstration of social co-benefits
- **Provide authentic impact narratives** — for corporate buyers seeking verifiable community-level impact

---

## How PACTO Supports Biocultural Credits

PACTO provides a verifiable participatory methodology for documenting that biocultural credits genuinely reflect community authorship. The framework:

1. Supports communities in **documenting and formalizing their own indicators** of ecological and cultural health through the Core Loop
2. Creates a **verifiable Evidence Trail** proving the community drove the agreement terms
3. Produces **formal legal instruments** (conservation easements, credit issuance terms) rooted in community voice
4. Supports **living contracts** that evolve with community needs and ecological conditions

Without verified community authorship, biodiversity credits that claim to be "biocultural" lack the distinguishing characteristic that gives the term its meaning.

---

## Value Flow Principles

1. **Community-first** — the largest share of credit revenue flows to the community
2. **Transparency** — all revenue splits are defined in the living contract and visible on-chain
3. **Community override** — the community can renegotiate terms through the amendment protocol
4. **No extraction without reciprocity** — every party in the value chain must provide demonstrable value to the community

## Open Questions

- What is the right community revenue share? Must be negotiated per community, not set centrally.
- How are credits priced when indicators are community-defined? Market discovery needed.
- Can the biocultural premium be quantified? Requires sales data from first PACTO-originated credits.
