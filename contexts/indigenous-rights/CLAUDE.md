# CLAUDE.md — Indigenous Rights Context

**For:** AI assistants working with indigenous rights content in this repository.

These guidelines supplement the root [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md) when working on files in `contexts/indigenous-rights/` or any content involving indigenous communities, FPIC, or pilot-specific information.

---

## Indigenous Rights-Specific Guidelines

1. **Never frame PACTO as a replacement for or improvement upon FPIC.** FPIC is an established right grounded in UNDRIP, ILO Convention 169, and regional jurisprudence including Sarayaku v. Ecuador (IACtHR, 2012). PACTO is implementation methodology. Use "participatory co-design," "community authorship," or "assembly-driven agreement" to describe PACTO's methodology. Never use "beyond FPIC," "FPIC is too low a bar," or "goes beyond consent."

2. **Ground in rights instruments.** When describing governance principles in indigenous contexts, reference UNDRIP, ILO Convention 169, Sarayaku v. Ecuador, and Ecuador's Constitution as the legal foundation. PACTO provides methodology; the rights are pre-existing.

3. **Never compare pilot communities.** Do not evaluate one community's process as more or less adequate than another's. Each community's governance is sovereign.

4. **Name partners with care.** When referencing implementation partners like Fundacion Pachamama, frame their contribution positively and specifically. Avoid language that could imply evaluation of their approach.

## Community Rights and Process Integrity

For any work involving indigenous knowledge, community processes, or pilot-specific content, review the [Process Integrity Checklist](process-integrity.md):

- [ ] Community authorship — was this generated through a PACTO process?
- [ ] Community endorsement — has the community reviewed and affirmed it?
- [ ] Data sovereignty — does it respect the community's data requirements?
- [ ] Attribution — is the contributing community properly credited?
- [ ] No extraction — does it add value for the community?
- [ ] Language integrity — has translation been verified by a community member?
- [ ] Power dynamics — does it risk creating dependency or externally-imposed frameworks?
- [ ] Reversibility — can the community withdraw this content?
- [ ] Rights grounding — does it reference the established legal foundation rather than framing PACTO as the source of rights?
