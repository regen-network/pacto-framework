# Governance Principles: Participatory Practice for Ecological Agreements

PACTO provides participatory methodology for implementing the right of communities and organizations to meaningful participation in decisions affecting their lands, livelihoods, and ecological agreements. Rather than replacing existing governance processes, PACTO instruments them with technology that makes deliberation more legible, actionable, and contractually binding — while keeping the community's voice as the primary source of authority.

> For the rights-grounded implementation in indigenous contexts — including UNDRIP, ILO Convention 169, and Sarayaku v. Ecuador — see [`contexts/indigenous-rights/governance-principles.md`](../contexts/indigenous-rights/governance-principles.md).

---

## Core Principle: Realizing the Full Intent of Consent

Consent is well established as a participatory right across governance contexts. The problem is implementation. In practice, consent is often reduced to a procedural minimum — a signature on terms drafted externally — rather than the substantive participatory process that meaningful governance requires.

PACTO addresses this implementation gap. It provides methodology for implementing consent at depth.

| Dimension | Procedural Implementation | Participatory Implementation (what PACTO methodology supports) |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Direction** | External to Community | Community to External |
| **Community role** | Reviewer | Author |
| **Authority over terms** | Limited (accept/reject) | Full (define/modify) |
| **Temporal scope** | Point-in-time consent | Ongoing co-design |
| **Evidence** | Consent documented | Entire process documented |
| **Amendment** | Renegotiation required | Living contract protocol |

---

## What PACTO Is Not

1. **PACTO is not a replacement for existing participatory rights.** Where legal frameworks establish rights to consent and participation, PACTO provides methodology for implementing those rights. They are not substitutes.
2. **PACTO does not create a new standard for consent.** Consent standards exist in applicable legal frameworks. PACTO provides tools for meeting them.
3. **PACTO is not required for governance to be legitimate.** Communities and organizations governed themselves before PACTO and will continue to do so without it. PACTO is an optional methodology.
4. **PACTO does not grant communities their voice.** Communities have always had voice, authority, and governance. PACTO provides technology that makes existing governance processes more legible to external parties — funders, regulators, and verifiers.
5. **PACTO does not define what matters.** Community values are defined by communities through their own frameworks. PACTO documents and structures these values for use in ecological agreements.

## Assembly Protocols

### Respect Existing Governance

PACTO does not introduce new governance structures. It instruments existing ones — assemblies, councils, community meetings, or any locally recognized decision-making forum.

The community decides: when to convene, who participates, what governance rules apply, what constitutes a quorum, how decisions are ratified.

### Voice-First Participation

Oral participation is the primary interface. PACTO accommodates:

- **Oral-only participants** — members who cannot read or write participate through voice, with equal weight
- **Multi-language deliberation** — internal discussion in community language, formal capture in shared administrative language
- **Visual and symbolic expression** — maps, drawings, gestures as valid input
- **Diverse knowledge traditions** — knowledge holders participate through oral traditions and cultural protocols

### Community Authority at Every Step

| Core Loop Step | Community Authority |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Assembly | Community convenes on its own terms |
| Voice Capture | Community approves capture methods and data handling |
| Synthesis | AI output is provisional; community word is final |
| Feedback | Community corrects, affirms, or rejects synthesis |
| Refinement | Community sets consensus threshold and iteration rules |
| Agreement | Community endorses final terms; can withdraw at any time |

---

## Data Sovereignty

1. **Community ownership** — assembly recordings, transcripts, and synthesis outputs belong to the community
2. **Community-defined access** — the community determines who can access their data
3. **Right to withdraw** — communities can request removal of their content at any time
4. **Storage self-determination** — communities choose where their data is stored
5. **No extraction without reciprocity** — every use of community data must add value for the community

## Power Dynamics Safeguards

The use of AI in participatory processes carries specific risks. PACTO mitigates them through:

- **Community override** at every AI-mediated step
- **Community endorsement** required before any knowledge is published
- **Language preservation** — translations flagged for community review
- **AI synthesis is always provisional** — community word is final
- **Voice-first design** — no requirement for digital literacy
- **Depth of deliberation serves community goals** — AI-augmented synthesis makes deliberation more accessible, not faster

## Co-governance

PACTO is not developed in isolation and imposed on communities. It is developed through co-governance with the communities and partners who use it.

- **Methodology evolution** — PACTO's Core Loop and governance principles evolve based on what communities and implementation partners learn through practice
- **Partner voice** — implementation partners (facilitators, organizations, legal counsel) contribute to PACTO's development based on their field experience
- **Community veto** — communities have the right to reject any aspect of PACTO methodology that does not serve their needs, and to adapt the methodology to their governance context
- **Trajectory toward community ownership** — the long-term goal is for PACTO infrastructure to be owned and operated by the communities it serves
- **Shared learning** — insights from PACTO processes are shared across the network (with community consent) to strengthen the methodology for all participants
