# The PACTO Core Loop

The Core Loop is the architectural backbone of PACTO. It is a 6-step iterative process that transforms community deliberation into formal, legally binding ecological agreements.

---

## The 6 Steps

### Step 1: Community Assembly

The community convenes using its **existing governance and decision-making processes** — indigenous assembly structures, tribal councils, community meetings, or whatever governance tradition the community already practices. PACTO does not introduce a new meeting format. It instruments an existing one.

Deliberation happens in mixed languages. In multilingual contexts, communities may discuss internally in their primary language, then shift to a shared administrative language for formal capture and external communication.

**Key principle:** The community decides when and how to convene. PACTO does not impose a schedule, quorum, or agenda.

### Step 2: Voice-to-Text Capture

Assembly proceedings are transcribed in real-time using voice-to-text tools. The transcription is the **primary source material** of community deliberation — the raw evidence of what the community said, in their words.

**Data sovereignty:** Transcripts are community property. Storage location and access controls are community-defined. Content-hash anchoring provides timestamping without requiring community data to live on-chain.

### Step 3: AI-Assisted Synthesis

AI processing extracts key themes, priorities, points of agreement, and points of tension from the transcript. This produces structured summaries:

1. **What the community said** about what they want
2. **How it relates** to the structure of agreements being developed
3. **Where there are open questions** or disagreements requiring further deliberation

**Quality safeguard:** AI synthesis is always provisional. It is subject to community correction in Step 4. The system never presents synthesis as authoritative — it is a structured mirror held up for the community to examine.

### Step 4: Feedback Presentation

The synthesized output is presented back to the community:

> "This is what you just said about what you want."

The community reviews, corrects, and affirms. This is the critical accountability step — it closes the loop between what the community actually said and what the technology captured and structured.

**Community actions at this step:**
- **Affirm:** "Yes, that captures what we said."
- **Correct:** "No, that's not what we meant."
- **Expand:** "You missed something important."
- **Reject:** "Start over."

### Step 5: Iterative Refinement

Steps 1-4 repeat in loops until the community reaches consensus. Each cycle sharpens the agreement terms, refines indicators, and resolves tensions.

**Consensus threshold:** This is a configurable, community-defined parameter. Different communities may define different thresholds — simple majority, supermajority, unanimous consent, or traditional decision-making protocols.

**Deliberative accessibility:** AI-augmented synthesis makes each assembly session more productive by structuring community input for review and refinement. The value is depth of engagement per session — not timeline compression. Communities set their own pace for iteration.

### Step 6: Agreement Generation

The final synthesis becomes the foundation for formal legal instruments:

- **Conservation easements** — land-use restrictions defined by community
- **Payment-for-ecosystem-service contracts** — payment terms linked to community-defined indicators
- **Credit issuance terms** — parameters for ecological credit generation
- **Living contracts** — adaptive agreements with community-controlled amendment protocols

**Key distinction:** These agreements are rooted in verbatim community voice, not lawyer-drafted boilerplate presented for sign-off. Legal professionals translate community intent into enforceable language — not the reverse.

## The Evidence Trail

PACTO creates a transparent, verifiable record of the participatory process itself — not just the outcome. This is MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification) of the participatory process:

| Evidence Layer | What Is Recorded |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Assembly convening** | Date, participants, governance structure used |
| **Voice capture** | Audio/transcript content hashes |
| **Synthesis outputs** | Each synthesis version with provenance |
| **Feedback records** | Community corrections, affirmations, rejections |
| **Iteration history** | Number of cycles, changes between versions |
| **Final agreement** | Endorsed agreement with community endorsement |

Funders and verifiers can see that the community actually drove the agreement terms, that deliberation happened, and that consent was genuine.

---

## The Surveillance Inversion

Most technology deployed in conservation contexts serves surveillance functions — monitoring compliance, tracking deforestation, verifying claims against communities. PACTO inverts this:

| Traditional Conservation Tech | PACTO Approach |
|------------------------------|---------------|
| Technology watches communities | Technology serves communities |
| Monitors compliance | Reflects community desires in contracts |
| Data flows to external verifiers | Data stays with community, shared by choice |
| Communities are subjects of monitoring | Communities are authors of agreements |

---

## Living Agreements

Agreements generated through PACTO are not static documents locked at signing. They are **living contracts** — ongoing, upgradeable agreements where communities make commitments that evolve over time. Amendment occurs through re-running the Core Loop for specific sections.

---

*The Core Loop is the foundational architecture of the PACTO framework. All other specifications build on these six steps.*
